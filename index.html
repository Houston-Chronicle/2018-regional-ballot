Governor: The governor of Texas is elected every four years. The governor's responsibilities include signing and vetoing bills passed by the state legislature, serving as commander-in-chief of the state's military, and convening special sessions of the Texas Legislature.

U.S. Senate: The U.S. Senate is one of two chambers that make up the United States Congress. There are two senators elected from each state, who write and vote on laws, approve presidential appointments and ratify treaties. Every two years, one-third of Senate seats are up for election. One of Texas' Senate seats is up for election 2018.

Lieutenant Governor: Texas elects its lieutenant governor every four years. As the second-highest executive office holder in Texas, the lieutenant governor is the presiding officer of the Texas Senate and is responsible for appointing Senate committees and casting votes in the case of a tie. The lieutenant governor becomes governor if the governor dies or leaves office.

Attorney General: Texas elects its attorney general every four years. The attorney general serves as the chief legal counsel for the state, and represents the state in litigation.

Comptroller: The Texas Comptroller elected every four years and is responsible for monitoring economic trends in the state, administrating and collecting taxes, and keeping accounts of state funds.

Land Commissioner: The Texas Land Commissioner, elected every four years, serves as the head of the General Land Office, which manages state lands, operates the Alamo, helps fund Texas public education, provides benefits to veterans, and helps the state recover from natural disasters.

Agriculture Commissioner: Texas elects an agriculture commissioner every four years. The agriculture commissioner heads the Department of Agriculture and oversees programs relating to the production and economic development of agriculture in the state. The agriculture commissioner also administers rural health programs.

Railroad Commissioner: Three railroad commissioners are elected to staggered six-year terms. Commissioners oversee the oil and gas industry in the state.
